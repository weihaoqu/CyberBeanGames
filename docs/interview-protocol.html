Opening Script

"Thank you for agreeing to this interview. I'm researching how building cybersecurity education games affects students' own learning and technical skills. This interview will take about 20–30 minutes. Your responses are confidential and will be reported using pseudonyms. You can skip any question or stop at any time. [If recording:] May I record this conversation for transcription purposes?"

Section 1: Background & Motivation ~5 min

1.1 Tell me about your background with cybersecurity before this course.
Probe: Courses taken? Personal interest? Career goals?
1.2 Why did you choose the specific cybersecurity concept your game teaches?
Probe: Was it something you were confident in, or something you wanted to learn more about?
1.3 Had you built a game or interactive learning tool before this project?

Section 2: Design Process & Learning ~8 min

2.1 Walk me through how you went from concept to finished game. What was your process?
Probe: Planning, prototyping, testing, iteration
2.2 At what point during development did you feel you understood the security concept most deeply?
Probe: Was there an "aha" moment? What triggered it?
2.3 Did you discover any gaps in your own knowledge while building the game?
Probe: How did you address those gaps? What resources did you use?
2.4 How did you decide what to simplify vs. keep technically accurate?
Probe: Can you give a specific example of a tradeoff you made?
2.5 Did playtesting reveal anything surprising about how people understood (or misunderstood) the concept?

Section 3: AI Tool Usage ~8 min

3.1 Which AI tools did you use, and how did you decide what to use AI for vs. do yourself?
Probe: Game logic? UI? Security content? Testing?
3.2 Can you describe a specific instance where the AI generated something incorrect about the security concept?
Probe: How did you detect the error? How did you fix it?
3.3 Did using AI tools change how deeply you engaged with the security material?
Probe: Did it help or hinder your learning? Did you just accept AI output or critically evaluate it?
3.4 If you were to do this project again, would you use AI differently? How?
3.5 Do you think the AI helped you build a better game, a worse game, or about the same as you would have built without it?
Probe: In terms of accuracy? Engagement? Polish?

Section 4: Reflection & Impact ~5 min

4.1 How does building a game compare to other ways you've learned cybersecurity concepts (lectures, labs, readings)?
4.2 Has this project changed how you think about cybersecurity education for non-technical audiences?
4.3 If a friend was about to start a similar project, what advice would you give them?
4.4 Is there anything else about this experience you'd like to share that I haven't asked about?
